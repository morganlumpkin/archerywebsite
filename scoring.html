Scoring in Archery

Posted by Someone on June 04, 2012  •  Comments (64)  •  Full article

This is About Scoring your archery card.

The highest you can score per shot is 10, so the highest you can score per round is 50. The highest you can score with all 6 rounds total is 300. Scoring a perfect 50 is really hard to do without practice and concentration.

Scoring in the outer white is 1 point and scoring in the inner white is 2 points. Missing the target entirely results in getting no points for that shot. scoring in the outer black results in getting a 3 and the inner black is a 4. This pattern will continue until you reach the center which is a 10x.

Marking these results on the score card is very simple. You just bubble in or write whatever score you made next to the round you are on. If it is on a bubble scanner then it will take less time to add up all of the scores. However, if yo have to count them all by hand the meets last longer due to the time it takes to count up all the points from all the teams that shot. That is the onlyl way to determine the winning team, and the top shooters from the winning team.
